# TMDX — TransMedics Group, Inc.

## Business Classification

High-growth medical device platform with integrated logistics services. TransMedics occupies a structurally unique position as the only FDA-approved portable warm-perfusion device for heart, lung, and liver transplants. The NOP (National OCS Program) layers recurring, case-level service revenue (disposables + logistics fees) on top of the device. This creates a recurring, volume-driven revenue model rather than lumpy capital-equipment revenue.

Tags: `#medical_device` `#high_growth` `#logistics_services` `#monopoly_position` `#margin_expansion`

## Valuation Lens

**Primary:** Forward EV/Revenue.
**Crosscheck:** Non-GAAP EPS × forward P/E.
**Rationale:** GAAP EPS is distorted by one-time tax benefits and stock-based compensation. EV/Revenue anchors to the NOP case-volume ramp, which is the most visible and credible revenue driver. Trailing P/E is unreliable until margin stability is established.

## Thesis

TransMedics is a monopoly in organ preservation with durable switching costs (NOP infrastructure). The investor is betting on US penetration, kidney as a fourth organ, European NOP launch, and margin recovery. The core risk is sustained gross margin compression and the residual Scorpion Capital governance overhang.

## Key Risks

1. Gross margin compression persists into 2027 from international NOP costs
2. US case volume growth moderates below 20% as penetration matures
3. Formal DOJ or CMS inquiry into NOP billing practices
4. $452M convertible notes due 2028 — refinancing risk in a tighter rate environment

## Key Catalysts

1. Q1 2026 gross margin recovery toward 59–60%+
2. OCS Kidney IDE filing progress in 2026
3. European NOP (Italy) case volumes above management guidance

## Lessons For Future Analysis

- **Medical device + logistics hybrid:** Use EV/Revenue as the primary anchor when the margin base is still oscillating from infrastructure investment. Switch to EV/EBITDA or P/E once margins stabilize.
- **Recurring case-revenue models:** Similar to SaaS NRR analysis — the key variable is cases-per-period growth, not just total revenue.
- **Governance overhang discounting:** Short-seller attacks that are formally investigated and cleared reduce but do not eliminate the multiple discount. The residual discount normalizes over 2–4 quarters if no regulatory action follows.
- **One-time tax benefit distortion:** When a deferred tax asset valuation allowance releases, it massively distorts GAAP EPS. Always isolate non-GAAP EPS for valuation work in the year of release.
