# MU — Micron Technology, Inc.

## Business Classification

Cyclical, capital-intensive memory semiconductor with a real AI/HBM structural upgrade. Micron is still a memory name first, so valuation must normalize through the cycle rather than capitalize peak-quarter EPS. The structural upgrade is that HBM4, multi-year customer commitments, and supply tightness beyond calendar 2026 have likely raised the through-cycle earnings floor versus old Micron setups.

Tags: `#semiconductor` `#memory` `#cyclical` `#ai_infrastructure` `#capex_heavy`

## Valuation Lens

**Primary:** FY2027 normalized non-GAAP EPS x target P/E.
**Cross-check:** Annualized Q3 EV/sales run-rate.
**Rationale:** Near-term EPS is too strong to use unadjusted, but FCF yield is also distorted because FY2026 capex is above $25B and FY2027 capex should step up again. Normalized EPS best captures the mix of cyclical normalization and structurally better AI economics.

## Verdict (2026-04-01)

**Fair to modestly cheap after the continued de-rating.**

At $321.80 (~25.5% below base fair value of $432), Micron offers significantly better risk-reward than at the March 28 analysis ($357.22). Bear downside is only ~8% while bull upside is ~80%. The sell-off from $471 to $322 was driven by profit-taking, capex anxiety, and Google TurboQuant compression fears, not by deteriorating fundamentals. Q3 guidance remains exceptional, HBM capacity is sold out through 2026, and the balance sheet is strong.

Thesis Status: **Intact**
Technical Entry: **Neutral to favorable (valuation improved further, momentum still negative but sell-off aggressive enough to create opportunity)**
Action State: **Watch for confirmation**

## Thesis

Micron is one of the cleanest listed ways to own the AI memory buildout. The investor is betting that HBM4, data-center storage, and strategic customer agreements have structurally raised Micron's earnings floor, while the market still applies an overly harsh memory-cycle multiple because it does not yet trust that higher floor through the next supply expansion. The TurboQuant compression narrative adds a new tail risk to inference memory demand but does not change HBM training demand dynamics where capacity is sold out.

## What the Price Implied

At $321.80, the market prices near the bear-base boundary, implying ~$36-40 FY2027 EPS at 8-9x P/E. This is more discount than the fundamentals currently warrant. The market has moved from pricing "strong but fading" to pricing "peak earnings, structural risks ahead."

## Key Modeling Decisions

- **FY2027 normalized EPS as primary anchor:** Near-term EPS is too strong to capitalize, but FCF yield is distorted by $25B+ capex. Normalized forward EPS best captures cyclical normalization plus structurally better AI economics.
- **8-10x P/E range:** Reflects Micron's improved but still cyclical earnings profile. Old Micron trough multiples ran 6-8x; the structural AI upgrade justifies 8-10x but not a growth-stock re-rating.
- **TurboQuant risk is tail, not central:** Inference compression is real at the margin but does not change training-dominated HBM demand in CY2026-2027 where supply is sold out.
- **Capex is the multiple governor, not the thesis killer:** Rising capex ($25B+ FY2026, higher FY2027) caps the multiple but does not break the thesis if supply discipline holds.

## Key Risks

1. Supernormal pricing and margins normalize faster than expected once supply constraints ease
2. FY2026/FY2027 capex and Tongluo-related cleanroom expansion recreate classic memory overbuild risk
3. Google TurboQuant or similar compression algorithms could structurally reduce AI memory demand per inference workload
4. Trade and geopolitical impacts were explicitly excluded from guidance
5. AI concentration around NVIDIA/HBM customers raises customer roadmap risk
6. PC and smartphone units can still decline low-double-digits in calendar 2026, hurting the non-AI mix

## Key Catalysts

1. Q3 FY2026 actual results validate the exceptional guide without margin fade
2. TurboQuant compression proves not to materially reduce HBM or DRAM purchasing volumes
3. Additional HBM commitments or multi-year strategic customer agreements for CY2027
4. Evidence that DRAM and NAND tightness really persists beyond calendar 2026
5. Sell-off stabilization with technical confirmation (RSI turn through EMA)
6. Proof that Tongluo and U.S. fab investment support returns instead of just capex volume

## Risk Lessons

- **Memory with structural AI content:** Use normalized forward EPS as the primary anchor, not spot-quarter EPS and not near-term FCF yield.
- **HBM visibility matters, but do not over-credit it:** Product ramps and SCAs justify a higher earnings floor, not an automatic growth-stock multiple.
- **Capex is the multiple governor:** When Micron's capex path rises above $25B and keeps climbing, the bear case shifts from demand collapse to overbuild and return compression.
- **Compression algorithm risk is worth monitoring, not panicking over:** TurboQuant-type claims can move the stock aggressively but the actual impact on hardware purchasing needs to be verified over multiple quarters.
- **Post-earnings sell-offs in strong cyclicals create opportunity if thesis is intact:** The move from $471 to $322 without fundamental deterioration widened the margin of safety from 17% to 25.5%.
