## What the Price Implied

At $332.68, the market is no longer just underwriting a healthy 2026 for Alphabet. The shares are pricing something closer to a high-end base or low-bull setup: resilient Search monetization, continued strong Cloud execution, and premium multiple tolerance through a US$175B-US$185B capex year. The debate is no longer whether the franchise is intact. The debate is whether capital productivity will be good enough to justify that premium without a new quarterly proof point.

## Key Modeling Decisions

- Used FY2026 forward EPS x target P/E as the primary lens because Alphabet is a mature, high-margin platform compounder and the real valuation question is per-share earnings power after the capex wave.
- Used trailing FCF yield only as a cross-check; at ~2.5%, it is still a discipline signal rather than the main valuation anchor because current AI infrastructure spending is intentionally suppressing near-term cash conversion.
- Set bear / base / bull fair values at $247.50 / $313.65 / $383.60 using $11.25 / $12.30 / $13.70 of FY2026 EPS and 22x / 25.5x / 28x target multiples.
- Treated capex productivity and multiple tolerance as the main valuation governor; the business is stronger than the old AI-cannibalization bear case, but the stock still trades above base fair value without a new fundamental disclosure.
- Treated Wiz completion as strategically supportive for Google Cloud, but not as a reason by itself to raise fair value before integration and returns are visible.

## Risk Lessons

- For Alphabet-type platform compounders, better AI positioning does not automatically make the stock cheap; business quality and entry price must stay separate.
- When the market is already paying roughly 26x forward earnings, upside depends less on proving product relevance and more on proving that the infrastructure build earns acceptable returns.
- When the stock sits above base fair value before a new quarter is reported, the analytical burden shifts even more toward current-price-implies work; what changed is expectations, not the business.
- Trailing FCF yield becomes more important as a warning signal when capex accelerates, even if it is not the right primary valuation method.
- Wiz, Waymo, and AI option value can support the bull case, but they should not be treated as free upside when the core equity already carries a premium multiple.
