## What the Price Implied

At $278.85, the market was already pricing something close to a 2026 bull-lite outcome. In practical terms, the stock implied about $53B-$55B of FY2026 revenue, AI GPU revenue in the mid-$20 billions, gross margin in the 57%-58% range, and a sustained low-30s earnings multiple before AMD had fully proven durable ROCm and platform economics.

## Key Modeling Decisions

- Used FY2026 non-GAAP EPS x target P/E as the primary lens because the current debate is about next-12-to-18-month AI shipment conversion, not distant terminal value.
- Used FY2026 EV / sales as the cross-check because software monetization, gross-margin conversion, and dilution are still being proven during the accelerator ramp.
- Set bear / base / bull fair values at $140 / $225 / $305 using $5.60 / $7.25 / $9.25 non-GAAP EPS and 25x / 31x / 33x target multiples.
- Gave real credit to Meta, Nutanix, Celestica, Samsung, and earlier OpenAI progress, but refused to capitalize multi-year announcements as if they had already fully landed in reported revenue.
- Treated ROCm adoption and shipment timing as the main multiple governor, not the existence of AI demand itself.

## Risk Lessons

- Strategic credibility can improve faster than auditable revenue conversion; that is exactly when valuation can outrun the model.
- For AMD, the key mistake is to confuse "credible second source" with "already deserving NVIDIA-adjacent economics."
- Warrant-based strategic alignment with large partners is supportive, but it also means per-share upside does not flow through one-for-one if milestones are hit.
- EV / sales is a necessary cross-check whenever AI hardware revenue is ramping faster than software proof and per-share earnings quality.
- If the stock is already near the bull fair value, better business quality is not enough to make the shares attractive.
