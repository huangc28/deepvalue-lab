## What the Price Implied

At $213.66, the market was already pricing something close to a bull-shaped FY2027 outcome. The stock effectively assumed sustained AI-era tool intensity, healthy service/support growth, manageable China risk, and a premium semicap multiple, leaving little room for a normal WFE digestion or multiple reset.

## Key Modeling Decisions

- Used FY2027 normalized non-GAAP EPS x target P/E as the primary lens because Lam is still cyclical even with a stronger installed-base service layer.
- Used annualized March-quarter guide EV/EBIT as the cross-check because a hot quarter and buyback support can make spot P/E look cleaner than the real valuation burden.
- Kept bear / base / bull at $128 / $172 / $226 to force the model to carry both operational strength and multiple-compression risk at the same time.
- Treated China exposure, tariffs, and export controls as the main multiple governor rather than just a later-section narrative risk.

## Risk Lessons

- AI-era semicap quality can be real while the stock is still too expensive; franchise quality and entry price must stay separate.
- A service mix in the high-30s percentage range improves the floor, but it does not eliminate cyclicality or justify an unconstrained premium multiple.
- When annualized guide EV/EBIT is already in the mid-30s, a lot of the AI narrative is probably already in the stock.
- China is not only a revenue risk for Lam. It is a valuation-cap risk because it can hit revenue, margins, and confidence simultaneously.
