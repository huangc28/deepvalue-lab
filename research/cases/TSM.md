## What the Price Implied

At $364.69, the market is already pricing in something close to management's near-30% 2026 revenue growth path plus disciplined execution on N2 and overseas fab dilution. The stock still is not pricing a demand collapse, but it also is no longer giving much room for easy multiple expansion without cleaner evidence that 2027 earnings power rises faster than the current capex drag.

## Key Modeling Decisions

- Used FY2026 forward ADR EPS x target P/E as the primary lens because the current question is earnings conversion after dilution, not long-dated narrative value.
- Kept trailing FCF yield only as a cross-check; at ~1.7%, it is too distorted by the current capex wave to anchor valuation.
- Set bear / base / bull fair values at $312 / $384.75 / $465 using $13.00 / $14.25 / $15.50 ADR EPS and 24x / 27x / 30x target multiples.
- Let margin dilution, not demand discovery, define the bear/base boundary: overseas fab and N2 costs now matter more than whether AI demand exists.

## Risk Lessons

- Once TSM already trades around the mid-20s on forward EPS, monthly revenue confirmation should move scenario confidence first, not the target multiple.
- Record capex and board-approved overseas funding strengthen the demand floor, but they also make raw FCF-based cheapness arguments much less decision-useful.
- The key mistake is to underwrite demand upside without separately underwriting dilution discipline; for this setup, margin conversion is the main risk variable.
- A constructive tape without a washed-out setup is not enough to turn a fair valuation into an aggressive entry.
