# MU Analysis

Date: 2026-03-18
Company: Micron Technology, Inc.
Ticker: MU
Point-in-time price: $462

## 1. Business Classification

**Cyclical / capital-intensive semiconductor — memory (DRAM + NAND) with a structural HBM growth layer.**

Micron is one of three global memory manufacturers alongside Samsung and SK Hynix. Memory is fundamentally a commodity cyclical business: revenue and margins swing with industry supply/demand and pricing cycles. However, HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) for AI accelerators is adding a technology-differentiated, supply-constrained layer that did not exist in prior memory cycles.

This classification matters because:
- Using peak P/E on cyclical peak earnings will overstate fair value
- The correct approach is normalized/mid-cycle earnings, with an adjustment for the structural HBM uplift
- EV/EBIT and normalized EPS are more appropriate than trailing P/E

## 2. Thesis

Micron is the primary Western beneficiary of an AI-driven memory supercycle. The core bet is that HBM demand structurally lifts Micron's earnings power above historical mid-cycle levels, while tight industry supply discipline sustains pricing longer than in previous memory cycles.

- HBM is sold out through calendar 2026 with binding agreements
- Cloud Memory revenue doubled YoY to $5.3B in FQ1, now 39% of revenue
- DRAM/NAND prices surged ~90% QoQ in early 2026
- FQ2 guidance implies 68% gross margin — a level never sustained in prior memory cycles
- Capex rising to $20B in FY2026 to support HBM capacity

## 3. Variant Perception

**The market is pricing MU as though HBM has permanently cured memory cyclicality. It probably hasn't.**

At ~14x FY2026 consensus EPS of $33, MU looks optically cheap. But the traditional memory cycle trap is that P/E looks cheapest at the peak. Historically, MU has traded at 8-12x at the top and appears expensive at the bottom when earnings collapse.

The variant is that the market may be underestimating the magnitude of eventual margin normalization. HBM genuinely raises the mid-cycle floor, but conventional DRAM and NAND (still ~60%+ of revenue) remain commodity markets that will normalize. A 68% gross margin is not sustainable — 45-50% is a more realistic mid-cycle with the HBM structural uplift.

Counter-variant: If HBM TAM grows from $35B (2025) to $100B (2028) as Micron forecasts, and Micron gains share toward 20-25%, the HBM revenue could reach $20-25B — enough to structurally anchor margins above 45% even in a conventional memory downturn.

## 4. Valuation Lens

**Primary: Normalized EPS x mid-cycle P/E.** This is the standard cyclical framework. For memory, the key is estimating sustainable mid-cycle margins, not peak-quarter margins.

**Cross-check: EV/EBIT on normalized operating income.** Enterprise multiples help control for the $11.8B debt and rising capex. At current EV ~$497B and FY26 consensus operating income of ~$40B, MU trades at ~12.4x current-year EV/EBIT — but this is on peak operating margins.

FCF yield is a poor cross-check here because capex is rising sharply ($20B in FY26), distorting near-term free cash flow relative to earnings power.

## 10. What The Current Price Implies

At $462, Micron trades at 13.8x FY2026 consensus EPS of $33.38. This appears cheap on a headline basis, but the question is whether $33 EPS is sustainable or cyclical peak.

Reverse-engineering the implied expectations:

| Implied Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| At 18x sustainable EPS | Implies ~$25.70 sustainable EPS — roughly the midpoint between base ($20.70) and bull ($32.50) |
| At 15x sustainable EPS | Implies ~$30.80 sustainable EPS — essentially pricing in that FY2026 peak earnings are the new normal |
| Implied gross margin to sustain $462 | ~50-55% on $70-75B revenue — well above historical mid-cycle but below current 68% guided |
| Implied vs. bear case | 198% premium — the market has fully priced out a normal cyclical downturn |
| Implied vs. base case | 23% premium — stock needs HBM to over-deliver to justify current price |

The market is pricing in a scenario between base and bull — that HBM has permanently elevated Micron's earnings power to ~$25-30 sustainable EPS and that a conventional memory downturn either won't happen or will be shallow. This is plausible but aggressive for a company that has historically experienced 60-80% earnings swings through cycles.

The key risk embedded in the current price: if gross margins normalize to 45% instead of staying at 50-55%, EPS drops to $18-22, and the stock needs to re-rate significantly lower.

## 11. Provisional Conclusion

**Fair, but at the upper edge — closer to slightly rich for a cyclical.**

MU at $462 sits 23% above the base-case fair value of $375 and requires a scenario between base and bull to justify. The near-term momentum is undeniable: FQ2 guidance at $18.7B revenue and 68% gross margin is extraordinary. HBM sold out through 2026 provides genuine earnings visibility.

However, paying 14x current-year peak earnings for a cyclical memory company assumes a margin of safety that doesn't exist. The stock has already moved from $62 to $462 in the past year — the AI memory story is fully in the price. Memory cycles have historically punished investors who extrapolate peak margins, and 68% gross margin is unprecedented for Micron.

For a new position: wait for a pullback into the $350-400 range, where the risk/reward aligns with the base case. For existing holders: the thesis is intact, but adding at $462 carries meaningful cyclical risk.

## 14. What To Monitor Next

1. FQ2 2026 results (tonight, March 18): Beat/miss vs. $18.7B revenue / $8.42 EPS guidance. FQ3 guidance will be more important than FQ2 actuals — any deceleration signals peak.
2. Gross margin trajectory: Watch whether 68% is the high-water mark. Any guide-down toward 55-60% signals margin normalization beginning.
3. DRAM/NAND spot pricing: After 90% QoQ surges, watch for price stabilization or reversal. TrendForce and DRAMeXchange pricing data are leading indicators.
4. Samsung HBM4 qualification status: Samsung catching up in HBM4 would pressure Micron's pricing power. Watch for Samsung qualification announcements with NVIDIA.
5. Hyperscaler AI capex updates: Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta capex guidance in April-May earnings. Any moderation would reduce the HBM demand outlook.
6. Micron's HBM market share in FQ2: Whether Micron holds or gains from the 11-21% range signals competitive positioning.
7. Industry capex discipline: All three memory makers are spending heavily. Watch for oversupply signals in conventional DRAM/NAND by late 2026.
