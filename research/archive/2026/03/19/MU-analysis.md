# MU Analysis

Date: 2026-03-19
Company: Micron Technology, Inc.
Ticker: MU (NASDAQ)
Point-in-time price: $461.73

## 1. Business Classification

**Cyclical memory semiconductor — in a structural AI-driven supercycle.**

Micron is the world's third-largest memory chipmaker behind Samsung and SK Hynix. Its revenue is split roughly 79% DRAM and 21% NAND. Historically, memory is one of the most brutally cyclical segments in semiconductors: commodity-like pricing, boom-bust capex cycles, and margins that swing from negative to 40%+ depending on supply-demand balance.

Why this classification matters for valuation: Memory companies have historically traded at single-digit P/Es during peak earnings because the market correctly discounts cycle normalization. The critical question for Micron today is whether HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) for AI has structurally changed the earnings profile enough to justify a higher through-cycle multiple — or whether current 75-81% gross margins are simply a more extreme version of the same peak that always mean-reverts.

## 2. Thesis

Micron is the primary Western beneficiary of the AI memory supercycle. HBM demand for AI training and inference (designed into NVIDIA Vera Rubin and successive architectures) has transformed Micron from a commodity memory supplier into a capacity-constrained provider of a strategic AI input. The company has locked in pricing and volume for its entire CY2026 HBM supply, is shipping HBM4 in production, and is guiding to 81% gross margins — a level never seen in memory industry history.

The investment thesis is that:
- HBM creates durable pricing power that is structurally different from conventional DRAM commodity cycles
- Supply discipline across the industry (cleanroom constraints, node migration difficulty) extends the tight market beyond CY2026
- Micron's capex investments ($25B+ in FY2026) position it to capture share in a $100B HBM TAM by CY2028
- At ~8-10x forward earnings, the market is still pricing significant cycle normalization — creating upside if the supercycle proves more durable than feared

## 3. Variant Perception

The market prices Micron at roughly 8-10x forward earnings, embedding a significant probability that current margins are peak and will compress sharply. This is the standard memory playbook — buy at high P/Es when earnings are depressed, sell at low P/Es when earnings are at cycle peaks.

The variant perception is that **this cycle is structurally different in duration and floor**. HBM is not a commodity: it requires advanced packaging (12-Hi and 16-Hi stacking), custom validation with specific GPU platforms (NVIDIA Vera Rubin, next-gen accelerators), and has multi-year supply agreements. Micron has already locked CY2026 HBM supply at agreed pricing, and HBM4E is targeting CY2027. This creates pricing visibility that conventional DRAM never had.

If the market is applying a traditional memory-cycle discount to what is actually a semi-structural shift in Micron's business mix and pricing power, the stock could be significantly undervalued. The key risk to this variant view is that Samsung and SK Hynix eventually flood HBM supply, compressing margins back toward mid-cycle norms.

## 4. Valuation Lens

**Primary: Forward P/E on FY2027 non-GAAP EPS.** This is the most decision-useful metric because Micron now has enough earnings visibility (locked HBM contracts, guided Q3, trajectory toward FY2027) to make forward EPS the anchoring metric. The critical variable is which P/E multiple is appropriate for a cyclical company potentially transitioning to a less cyclical profile.

**Cross-check: EV/EBITDA.** This provides a capital-structure-neutral view and captures Micron's net cash position ($3.8B net cash as of Q2 FY2026). However, EV/EBITDA alone can understate the capex burden during heavy investment cycles ($25B+ in FY2026), so it is a secondary lens, not primary.

Why not FCF yield: FCF is heavily distorted by the massive capex cycle. Q2 FY2026 FCF was $6.9B, but capex was $5.0B and rising. FY2026 capex will exceed $25B. FCF yield understates the true economic earning power during this investment phase and overstates it if you ignore the capex that sustains the franchise.

Sequential guidance implies 40% revenue growth and 600bps gross margin expansion in a single quarter.

**Model impact:**
- **Revenue:** FY2026 full-year revenue is now tracking well above $100B. FY2027 revenue estimates need major upward revision.
- **Margin:** 81% guided GM is unprecedented in memory history. If sustainable even partially, normalized margins are much higher than historical averages.
- **Multiple:** The strength of the guide could support a higher target P/E if the market begins to believe the cycle is structural, not just peak.

### Event 3: HBM4 Volume Shipments and Full CY2026 Supply Lock (March 18, 2026)
Micron is shipping HBM4 36GB 12-Hi for NVIDIA Vera Rubin. Entire CY2026 HBM supply is contracted at agreed pricing and volume. HBM4 16-Hi (48GB) sampled. HBM4E targeting CY2027.

**Model impact:**
- **Revenue visibility:** CY2026 HBM revenue is essentially locked. This reduces near-term downside risk.
- **Margin:** Contracted pricing provides margin floor for the next 9+ months.
- **Thesis quality:** Strengthens the thesis that HBM is not a commodity — long validation cycles and locked contracts create pricing stickiness.

### Event 4: Capex Raised to $25B+ for FY2026, FY2027 "Step Up Meaningfully" (March 18, 2026)
Capex investment prioritizes HBM capacity and 1-gamma node output.

**Model impact:**
- **Capex/FCF:** Higher capex reduces near-term FCF despite record earnings. FY2027 capex could be $30B+.
- **Bear case risk:** If the cycle turns while capex is elevated, Micron could face the classic memory trap of overcapacity at the wrong time. This is the primary risk that caps the multiple.
- **Bull case support:** If demand sustains, this capex builds the capacity to capture a $100B HBM TAM by CY2028.

### Event 5: Tariff Uncertainty Not in Guidance (March 18, 2026)
Management explicitly excluded potential tariff impacts from Q3 guidance.

**Model impact:**
- **Scenario probability:** Adds tail risk to all scenarios. Tariffs on memory imports could compress margins or shift demand patterns.
- **Bear case:** Tariff escalation is a bear case amplifier, though Micron has shown ability to pass through costs via surcharges.

### Event 6: Supply Constraints — Customers Receiving 50-67% of Demand (March 18, 2026)
Key customers are only getting half to two-thirds of requested supply.

**Model impact:**
- **Revenue:** Demand significantly exceeds supply — revenue is supply-constrained, not demand-constrained.
- **Multiple:** Supply constraints are pricing-power evidence. Supports higher-for-longer margin thesis.
- **Bear case timing:** Even if a downturn comes, the demand backlog provides a buffer before margins compress.

## 7. Bear Case

**Operating assumptions:**
- Memory cycle peaks by late CY2026; HBM pricing softens as Samsung and SK Hynix ramp competitive HBM4 products
- Conventional DRAM/NAND enters downcycle as AI capex moderates and enterprise refresh stalls
- FY2027 revenue: ~$100B (significant deceleration from FY2026 run-rate)
- Non-GAAP gross margin compresses to ~50% (still above historical average due to HBM mix, but well below current 75-81%)

**Valuation assumptions:**
- FY2027 non-GAAP EPS: ~$38
- Target P/E: 8x (market applies full cyclical discount as margins visibly compress)

**Fair value: ~$304**

**What must be true:**
- Samsung and SK Hynix ramp HBM4 supply faster than expected, breaking Micron's pricing power
- AI capex growth decelerates or pauses (hyperscaler budget fatigue)
- Conventional DRAM/NAND pricing turns negative
- Micron's elevated capex ($25B+) creates overcapacity at the worst time
- Tariff escalation or geopolitical disruption hits demand

- FY2027 revenue: ~$125B (growth moderates from the parabolic FY2026 trajectory)

**What must be true:**

- HBM4E extends Micron's technology lead

## 10. What The Current Price Implies

At $461.73 with 1.15B diluted shares, the market cap is ~$531B. Backing into implied expectations:

- **At 10x P/E** (base-case multiple): implied FY2027 EPS of ~$46 — roughly in line with stale consensus ($45.34) but well below our base case of $54. The market appears to be pricing base-case earnings at a standard memory-cycle multiple, essentially saying "we believe consensus but don't believe earnings will beat."
- **At 8x P/E** (bear-case multiple): implied FY2027 EPS of ~$58 — which would be an optimistic earnings outcome at a deeply discounted multiple. This interpretation suggests the market expects very high earnings but discounts their durability.

The most realistic read: **the market is pricing Micron at approximately 8.5x its realistic FY2027 base-case EPS**, embedding a ~40-50% probability that the memory cycle normalizes within 12-18 months. This is classic memory-stock behavior — the market reflexively applies a peak-cycle discount.

The embedded assumptions are:
- Implied FY2027 EPS: ~$46-54
- Implied FY2027 gross margin: ~55-60% (significant compression from current 75-81%)
- Implied FY2027 revenue: ~$110-125B
- Implied HBM pricing: stable but not expanding

**Key judgment:** The current price gives you base-case earnings at a cyclical discount. You are not paying for the bull case. The risk-reward is asymmetric to the upside if HBM proves more durable than the market's memory-cycle template assumes — but the downside to $300 is real if this turns out to be another classic memory peak.

## 11. Provisional Conclusion

**Fair, with asymmetric upside if the HBM thesis holds.**

At $462, Micron trades at ~8.5x realistic FY2027 base-case EPS of ~$54, representing roughly 15% discount to the $540 base fair value. This is not cheap enough to be a high-conviction accumulation zone — the stock has already rallied 21.6% in two weeks and sits at its 52-week high. But it is not expensive either, given the extraordinary earnings trajectory (Q3 guide: $19.15 EPS on $33.5B revenue, with free cash flow expected to double sequentially).

The market is pricing Micron using the old memory-cycle framework: low P/E at peak earnings. The variant bet is that HBM contract structure, supply constraints, and AI structural demand make this cycle longer and the earnings floor higher than history suggests. If that is correct, the stock could trade toward $540-840. If the old playbook is right and margins mean-revert, the stock could trade down toward $300.

**My judgment:** The HBM structural shift is real but not fully proven. CY2026 supply is locked and visible. CY2027-2028 depends on whether AI capex sustains and whether Samsung/SK Hynix maintain pricing discipline. The risk-reward favors accumulation on pullbacks toward $380-420 rather than chasing at $462 post a massive earnings gap-up. A 15% discount to base fair value is not sufficient margin of safety for a cyclical business at record margins.
