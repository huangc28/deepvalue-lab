# TSM Analysis

Date: 2026-03-19
Company: Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company
Ticker: NYSE: TSM
Point-in-time price: US$345.98 per ADR (NYSE close on 2026-03-18)

## 1. Business Classification

TSMC is a high-quality, capital-intensive semiconductor infrastructure business with cyclical end-demand exposure. It is not a commodity foundry. That distinction matters because the valuation anchor should be forward earnings power with a capital-intensity and geopolitical haircut, not a trough-cycle commodity multiple.

Advanced nodes below 7nm contributed 74% of 2025 wafer revenue, HPC was 58% of 2025 revenue, and TSMC now commands approximately 70% of the global foundry market (up from 64.4% in 2024). This dominance and node leadership make earnings durability and technology positioning more decision-useful than a pure cycle call.

## 2. Thesis

The investable thesis is that TSMC has become the bottleneck owner of leading-edge compute and advanced packaging, enabling it to compound earnings faster and longer than a typical foundry, even as it absorbs a massive 2026-2027 capex cycle.

At the current price, an investor is underwriting that:

- AI and HPC demand remain strong enough to offset overseas fab dilution, N2 ramp inefficiency, and any tariff-related weakness in consumer end markets
- The recent US-Taiwan tariff reduction (20% to 15%) incrementally improves the cost structure for US-bound shipments and supports non-AI demand stability
- TSMC's infrastructure-like earnings base justifies a premium multiple versus old foundry comps

If that balance holds, the stock is reasonable. If margin dilution bites harder than the market expects, downside opens quickly because the stock no longer trades like a cheap cyclical.

## 3. Variant Perception

The market is no longer missing TSMC's quality, but it may still be underestimating how infrastructure-like the earnings base has become while simultaneously overestimating how much near-term multiple expansion is left.

The more nuanced variant: the recent tariff relief and near-70% market share are incrementally positive, but they do not meaningfully change the core tension. TSMC is a better business than old foundry framing suggests, but less obviously cheap than AI enthusiasm implies. The stock at ~$346 trades closer to the base case than the bear case, which means investors are already paying for solid execution. The upside from here requires either cleaner-than-guided margin execution or sustained AI demand surprise — both possible but not guaranteed.

My judgment: the market's main blind spot is not TSMC's quality (everyone sees it) but rather the tendency to dismiss capital intensity and overseas dilution as "already known." These factors are known but may not yet be fully reflected in the multiple the market is willing to sustain if margins disappoint even modestly.

## 4. Valuation Lens

Primary lens: forward ADR P/E.
Secondary cross-check: trailing FCF yield, used cautiously.

Forward P/E fits best because TSMC is profitable, net-cash, and structurally differentiated. EV/EBITDA is less helpful here because it can flatter a business spending heavily to preserve its moat. Spot FCF yield is directionally useful but too distorted by the US$52-56 billion 2026 capex program to serve as the main anchor.

The FCF yield cross-check: on trailing 2025 numbers, FCF yield is only about 1.7%, reflecting heavy investment. This is not alarming given the investment phase, but it confirms that investors are paying for future earnings, not current cash generation.

## 6. Recent News And News-To-Model Translation

1. **US-Taiwan tariff cut from 20% to 15% on qualifying tech trade** (reported mid-March 2026).
   News-to-model: This is modestly positive for margins and demand. For TSMC's US-bound shipments (roughly 65% of revenue by customer geography), a 5 percentage point tariff reduction eases cost pressure on customers and supports volumes in price-sensitive non-AI segments. It does not directly boost TSMC's ASPs, but it improves the demand environment for consumer/auto end markets that were under tariff pressure. Net impact: slight upward adjustment to base-case net margin (~+0.3pp) and slight reduction in bear-case probability.

2. **TSMC confirmed ~70% global foundry market share in 2025** (Taipei Times, 2026-03-14).
   News-to-model: This reinforces the structural thesis — TSMC's competitive moat is widening, not narrowing. Samsung fell to 7.2% share. This supports the premium multiple versus peers but is already largely priced in. Net impact: multiple support (prevents de-rating), no direct EPS change.

3. **New Tainan fab planned for 2028 completion** (Taipei Times, 2026-03-05).
   News-to-model: Confirms management is investing for multi-year demand. Creates approximately 1,400 direct jobs. This extends the capital intensity story through 2028-2029, which means FCF yield will remain suppressed for longer than some investors may expect. Net impact: slight upward adjustment to long-term revenue capacity, slight downward pressure on medium-term FCF.

4. **Q4 2025 results and Q1 2026 guidance remain the most recent earnings data** (unchanged from prior analysis).
   Q1 2026 guidance: revenue US$34.6-35.8B, gross margin 63-65%, operating margin 54-56%. Analyst consensus Q1 EPS: ~$3.26 per ADR. Earnings report due April 16, 2026.
   News-to-model: unchanged since last analysis. Jan-Feb 2026 revenue up 29.9% YoY continues to support the near-30% 2026 growth framework.

5. **Management warning on overseas fab and N2 margin dilution** (unchanged from Q4 earnings call).
   Overseas fab ramp dilutes gross margin by 2-3% initially, 3-4% later. N2 ramp dilutes 2026 gross margin by 2-3%.
   News-to-model: This caps aggressive margin assumptions and keeps the bear case alive even if revenue growth remains strong.

6. **Board-approved US$44.96B capital appropriation and US$30B TSMC Global injection** (2026-02-10, unchanged).
   News-to-model: Reinforces rising capital intensity. Confirms management conviction in multi-year demand but also confirms FCF suppression continues.

## 10. What The Current Price Implies

At US$345.98, the stock implies approximately US$13.1 of forward ADR EPS at a 26-27x multiple, which maps to roughly US$154-155 billion of 2026 revenue at a 44% net margin, or approximately 26% growth versus 2025. This is a strong outcome — above the bear case and solidly in base-case territory, though not pricing in the full base case.

Put differently, the current price underwriting sits between bear and base, closer to base than to stress. The market is giving TSMC credit for sustained AI demand and above-industry growth. What it is not fully paying for is a clean bull path with contained dilution.

Implied facts at current price:
- Implied 2026 EPS: ~US$13.1/ADR
- Implied 2026 revenue: ~US$154-155B
- Implied 2026 net margin: ~44%
- Implied 2026 revenue growth: ~26%
- Implied forward P/E: ~26-27x

The tariff reduction has slightly improved the implied picture (the same price now buys a marginally better fundamental backdrop), but the core setup remains: reasonable if base case holds, not deeply undervalued.

## 11. Provisional Conclusion

TSM looks **fair to slightly attractive**, not cheap.

Business quality is elite and the 2026 demand backdrop is strong enough to justify a premium valuation. The recent US-Taiwan tariff cut is incrementally positive for the non-AI demand base and supports the case that the worst of the tariff headwind may be easing. However, the current price already assumes continued high growth and does not leave a large margin of safety against the two variables most likely to cap returns: margin dilution from global expansion and N2 ramp, and any further softening in non-AI demand.

The stock offers approximately 10% upside to base fair value and approximately 31% upside to bull fair value, against approximately 14% downside to bear fair value. At $346, the risk/reward is acceptable but not compelling for aggressive new positioning.

My underwriting judgment: the hidden support is that TSMC is more infrastructure-like than old foundry labels imply, and the improving tariff environment provides a modest tailwind that most sell-side models may not yet fully capture. The key risk is that investors treat these positives as permission to ignore capital intensity and dilution. That leads me to "reasonable if base case holds," not "deeply undervalued."

## 12. Thesis Status

**Intact.**

The core thesis — TSMC owns the most important manufacturing bottlenecks in advanced compute — is reinforced by the latest data: near-70% market share, sustained AI demand, Jan-Feb revenue tracking near 30% growth, and an improving (not worsening) tariff environment. There is no deterioration in the business story. I am not moving to watch because business evidence remains strong. The reason I am not more aggressive is valuation discipline, not a weakening business.

## 14. What To Monitor Next

1. **Q1 2026 earnings on April 16**: Whether revenue and gross margin land near the high end of the US$34.6-35.8B / 63-65% guidance range. A high-end print would confirm the year is tracking above market expectations.
2. **Monthly revenue for March 2026** (expected around April 10): Whether the Jan-Feb YoY growth pace of ~30% is sustained or whether it decelerates.
3. **Overseas fab and N2 margin dilution**: Whether management provides updated dilution estimates in the Q1 call — particularly whether the 2-3% overseas impact is tracking better or worse than initial guidance.
4. **Advanced packaging capacity utilization**: Whether CoWoS and SoIC remain supply-constrained in TSMC's favor, or whether capacity begins to loosen.
5. **Tariff environment evolution**: Whether the 20% → 15% cut is a one-off or the beginning of a further reduction trend. Further tariff easing would be modestly positive for non-AI demand and multiple support.
6. **Non-AI demand trajectory**: Whether smartphone, auto, and IoT demand stabilizes or continues to soften, which affects the breadth of TSMC's revenue base beyond the AI pillar.
