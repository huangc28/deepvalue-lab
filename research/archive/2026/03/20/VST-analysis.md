# VST Analysis

Date: 2026-03-20
Company: Vistra Corp.
Ticker: VST
Point-in-time price: ~$163.69

## 1. Business Classification

Vistra is best viewed as hybrid contracted/merchant power infrastructure, not as a plain regulated utility and not as a pure merchant commodity trade. The company pairs a large retail book with generation assets across gas, nuclear, coal, solar, and storage, so cash flow is partly stabilized by customer load and long-term contracts, but still exposed to power-market curves, outages, and capital allocation.

That classification matters because the right valuation lens is enterprise-value based. Leverage, asset intensity, and acquisition-driven growth make EV / EBITDA more decision-useful than headline P/E, while cash conversion remains important enough to justify a free-cash-flow-before-growth cross-check.

## 2. Thesis

The bull case on Vistra is that rising power demand in ERCOT and PJM is making its gas and nuclear fleet more strategically valuable, and management is steadily converting that optionality into higher-quality contracted cash flows.

The stock is interesting because:

- the integrated retail-plus-generation model gives Vistra a better monetization path than a standalone merchant generator
- long-duration nuclear PPAs and gas-asset acquisitions can raise base profitability, not just cyclical upside
- disciplined buybacks and 60%+ medium-term EBITDA-to-FCFbG conversion make the equity story more than a commodity-price call
- the main underwriting risk is that investors may already be capitalizing a near-bull mix of power-demand strength, contract wins, and acquisition execution

## 3. Variant Perception

The market is no longer missing the AI-power and nuclear-demand story. The more realistic variant question is whether investors are now paying too early for a structurally higher earnings base before Cogentrix is closed, before more gas and nuclear contracts are signed, and before the longer-dated hedge roll fully proves out.

My judgment is that the first leg of the re-rating was deserved, but the stock now needs execution to keep working. This is not an unloved utility; it is a partially de-risked infrastructure winner whose next return leg depends on converting narrative into durable contracted EBITDA.

## 4. Valuation Lens

Primary lens: EV / EBITDA on normalized 2027 earnings power.

Cross-check: price / adjusted free cash flow before growth (P / FCFbG).

Why this fits:

- debt and capital intensity materially affect equity value, so enterprise value matters
- management itself frames the business around adjusted EBITDA and adjusted FCFbG
- P/E is less clean here because acquisition accounting, hedging, tax items, and capital structure can distort comparability
- FCFbG is a useful cross-check because Vistra is explicitly managed for cash allocation, buybacks, dividends, and deleveraging

## 5. Current Valuation Snapshot

Using a point-in-time share price of about $163.69 on March 20, 2026 and equity value of about $55.2B, Vistra trades at an enterprise value of roughly $72.5B after adding year-end 2025 net debt of about $17.4B.

Key valuation anchors:

- ~10.1x EV / 2026 EBITDA midpoint using $7.2B midpoint guidance
- ~9.5x EV / 2027 EBITDA midpoint opportunity if management's $7.4B-$7.8B 2027 range is broadly achieved
- ~12.8x P / 2026 FCFbG midpoint, or about 7.8% FCFbG yield on the equity

Balance sheet context matters but is no longer a thesis-breaker. Management is targeting roughly 2.3x net debt / EBITDA by year-end 2027, which supports the idea that Vistra is moving from leveraged cyclical equity toward infrastructure-like cash allocator. The problem is that the stock price already gives meaningful credit for that transition.

## 6. Recent News And News-To-Model Translation

### Q4 and full-year 2025 results plus 2026 guidance

Vistra reported 2025 ongoing operations adjusted EBITDA of $6.167B and ongoing operations FCFbG of $3.741B. For 2026, management guided to $6.8B-$7.6B of EBITDA and $3.925B-$4.725B of FCFbG.

News-to-model effect:

- raises the base-case earnings floor versus a plain merchant-power framing
- supports the idea that 2026 is not a fade year, but a step-up year
- increases confidence that buybacks and debt reduction remain funded internally

### Meta nuclear PPAs

In January 2026, Vistra announced a long-term strategic relationship with Meta tied to more than 2.6 GW from its Illinois nuclear plants, with additional capacity from uprates over time. The Q4 presentation also highlighted PJM nuclear agreements and estimated meaningful FCFbG accretion.

News-to-model effect:

- improves contracted revenue visibility and raises base-case durability
- supports a better mix of contracted versus merchant EBITDA
- lowers the probability that the market should value Vistra like a short-duration power spike story
- also caps some pure merchant upside, because higher-quality contracted cash flow deserves steadier but not unlimited multiple expansion

### Cogentrix acquisition

Vistra announced the acquisition of roughly 5.5 GW of modern natural gas generation from Cogentrix at about 7.25x 2027 adjusted EBITDA contribution, funded with assumed debt, cash, and 5 million shares. Management expects mid-single-digit FCFbG accretion in 2027 and high-single-digit average accretion over 2027-2029.

News-to-model effect:

- increases long-term EBITDA base and strengthens exposure to attractive power markets
- adds more operating leverage to load growth and data-center demand
- but keeps integration, regulatory approval, and leverage discipline as live downside variables
- reinforces that Vistra should not be valued as a simple low-risk utility

### Hedge profile and cash-conversion framework

Management disclosed expected generation hedge percentages of about 100% for 2026, 84% for 2027, and 58% for 2028, while reiterating an expectation to convert 60%+ of adjusted EBITDA to FCFbG over the medium term.

News-to-model effect:

- near-term 2026 cash flows are more visible than the stock's volatility may suggest
- the longer-dated upside case depends more on 2028 roll-off, new contracts, and market curves
- makes it harder to argue the stock is cheap simply because spot power markets are strong today

## 7. Bear Case

Operating assumptions:

- 2026 lands near the low end of guidance and 2027 EBITDA settles around $6.8B
- Cogentrix closes but contributes less than hoped, or integration plus financing tempers equity accretion
- additional nuclear and gas contracts arrive slower than the market expects
- 2028 hedge roll and power curves do not support another upward revision cycle

Valuation assumptions:

- apply 8.6x EV / EBITDA to ~$6.8B of EBITDA
- implied enterprise value: ~$58.5B
- less net debt of ~$17.4B
- implied equity value: ~$41.1B
- using roughly 337M diluted shares, fair value is about $122 per share

What must be true:

Vistra would need to look more like a fully priced cyclical power name than a steadily improving infrastructure compounder. That likely means weaker contract cadence, less favorable power-market follow-through, or a renewed market focus on leverage and execution risk.

## 8. Base Case

Operating assumptions:

- management broadly achieves the 2027 EBITDA midpoint opportunity range, or about $7.6B
- Meta and other signed contracts do what management says they should do
- Cogentrix closes on schedule and cash conversion remains around the 60% medium-term framework
- the market continues to treat Vistra as improving-quality power infrastructure, but not as a premium growth utility

Valuation assumptions:

- apply 10.0x EV / EBITDA to ~$7.6B of EBITDA
- implied enterprise value: ~$76.0B
- less net debt of ~$17.4B
- implied equity value: ~$58.6B
- using roughly 337M diluted shares, fair value is about $174 per share

What must be true:

Vistra needs to keep delivering on the current strategic script: strong retail execution, stable fleet operations, credible deleveraging, and more evidence that contract wins raise base profitability rather than just pull forward excitement.

## 9. Bull Case

Operating assumptions:

- Vistra exits 2027 with earnings power closer to $8.3B of EBITDA
- Cogentrix integration goes cleanly, load growth stays strong in ERCOT and PJM, and more gas or nuclear contracts are signed
- 2028 hedge roll and contracting support a view that the current 2027 framework is still conservative

Valuation assumptions:

- apply 10.8x EV / EBITDA to ~$8.3B of EBITDA
- implied enterprise value: ~$89.6B
- less net debt of ~$17.4B
- implied equity value: ~$72.3B
- using roughly 337M diluted shares, fair value is about $214 per share

What must be true:

The market would need proof that Vistra deserves to be capitalized as a structurally tighter, more contracted, more durable power-infrastructure platform rather than as a late-cycle beneficiary of AI electricity enthusiasm.

## 10. What The Current Price Implies

At roughly $163.69, the stock already prices something close to the current base case, not the bear case. The market is underwriting about 10.1x 2026 EBITDA midpoint and roughly 12.8x 2026 FCFbG midpoint, which effectively assumes Vistra can deliver most of the 2027 earnings step-up, close and integrate Cogentrix without a major stumble, and keep turning merchant optionality into contract-backed cash flow. What does not appear fully priced is a second major upside leg from additional nuclear or gas contracting, materially tighter 2028 market rolls, or a much higher terminal multiple. In other words, the market is paying for good execution and some strategic scarcity, but not yet for an outright bull case.

## 11. Provisional Conclusion

Vistra looks fair, with a slight bias toward attractive only if the stock gives you a better pullback. The business quality and earnings visibility are improving, and the AI-linked power-demand story is real. But the stock is not obviously cheap because the current price already discounts something close to management's 2027 EBITDA opportunity and a steady deleveraging path.

My practical conclusion is this: VST is a name worth owning or monitoring, but not one where today's price clearly misreads reality. I would be more interested on weakness toward the low-$150s or below, where the market would stop charging you nearly full price for the current strategic playbook.

## 12. Thesis Status

Intact.

The core thesis that Vistra owns increasingly strategic gas and nuclear infrastructure, and can convert power-market tailwinds into higher-quality cash flows, is still holding. What changed is not the business thesis but the valuation cushion. The stock has become less forgiving.

## 13. Technical Entry Status

Neutral.

A March 19, 2026 technical snapshot showed RSI around 56, price above the 20-day and 50-day moving averages, but still below the 200-day moving average. That is not a washed-out setup, and it is not an obviously euphoric blow-off either. Since the valuation is only fair, not cheap, the chart does not upgrade the setup into a strong entry zone.

## 14. What To Monitor Next

- whether Cogentrix closes on the expected timeline and what the first clean integration markers look like
- whether Vistra signs more long-duration gas or nuclear contracts beyond the already announced deals
- 2027 and 2028 hedge rolls, especially how much incremental EBITDA survives beyond today's contracted base
- whether management can keep net debt / EBITDA moving toward the ~2.3x year-end 2027 target
- plant reliability and outage performance, because infrastructure quality only deserves a better multiple if operations stay dependable
- nuclear PTC economics and any policy or IRS interpretation changes that affect the support level on nuclear cash flows
