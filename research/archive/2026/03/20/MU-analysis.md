# MU Analysis

Date: 2026-03-20
Company: Micron Technology, Inc.
Ticker: MU (NASDAQ)
Point-in-time price: $444.27 (March 19, 2026 close)

## 1. Business Classification

**Cyclical memory semiconductor with a real AI/HBM structural upgrade, but still ultimately governed by supply-demand cycles and normalized earnings.**

Micron is still a memory company first. That matters because memory names should not be valued off peak-quarter earnings as if they were software compounders. However, the mix is no longer purely commodity DRAM/NAND: HBM, strategic customer agreements, and supply constraints have raised near-term visibility and likely raised the through-cycle margin floor versus old Micron cycles.

Why this classification matters for valuation: the right lens is **forward normalized EPS with explicit cycle normalization assumptions**, not trailing P/E on peak earnings and not raw FCF yield, because current free cash flow is distorted by capex above $25 billion and current earnings are amplified by unusually tight supply and HBM mix.

## 2. Thesis

Micron is one of the clearest listed ways to own the AI memory buildout, but the stock is no longer priced like a distressed cyclical. The thesis today is not "the market ignores AI"; it is that the market still discounts Micron using the old memory-cycle playbook more aggressively than is justified by HBM contract visibility, product roadmap progress, and industry supply constraints.

Core thesis:
- HBM and data-center memory demand have made Micron's earnings stream more visible than in prior cycles.
- Strategic customer agreements and full CY2026 HBM supply commitments reduce near-term revenue and margin downside.
- Tight supply conditions in both DRAM and NAND beyond calendar 2026 support a higher earnings floor than traditional memory bears assume.
- The main underwriting risk is not demand this quarter. It is whether elevated capex and future competitor supply eventually recreate the classic memory overbuild.

## 3. Variant Perception

The market still appears to be underwriting a meaningful normalization in Micron's earnings power even after a blowout fiscal Q2 and an extraordinary Q3 guide. That skepticism is understandable: memory stocks have trained investors to sell low-P/E peak earnings. The possible misread is that this cycle has more contractual visibility and more structural AI content than prior cycles, so the earnings collapse embedded in a traditional memory discount may be too severe.

The counterpoint is important: this is no longer a hated stock. After the run into and through earnings, Micron is not obviously mispriced the way it was when the market doubted the HBM ramp entirely. The variant is moderate, not heroic.

## 4. Valuation Lens

**Primary lens: FY2027 normalized non-GAAP EPS x target P/E.**

That fits Micron best because the business is still cyclical, but management now has enough visibility from HBM, data-center demand, and Q3 guidance to frame a forward earnings range instead of relying on spot-quarter numbers alone.

**Cross-check: run-rate EV/sales, used only as a sanity check.**

At the current enterprise value, Micron trades around **3.8x annualized Q3 revenue run-rate**. I use that only to check whether the equity already assumes an extreme AI multiple. It does not replace earnings-based valuation, and I am explicitly not using FCF yield as a primary cross-check because FY2026 capex above $25 billion and a likely step-up in FY2027 distort the signal.

## 6. Recent News And News-To-Model Translation

### Event 1: Fiscal Q2 2026 results materially reset the earnings base
Micron reported **$23.86B revenue, 74.9% non-GAAP gross margin, and $12.20 non-GAAP EPS** in fiscal Q2.

**News-to-model translation:**
- **Revenue:** H1 FY2026 revenue plus Q3 guidance already puts full-year revenue on a trajectory that is far above Micron's prior-cycle earnings base.
- **Margin:** A mid-70s gross margin confirms that HBM and tight supply are not just volume stories; they are pricing and mix stories.
- **Scenario probability:** This pushes probability away from a near-term hard-reset bear case and toward base/bull outcomes.

### Event 2: Q3 guidance is the clearest evidence that this is more than a one-quarter spike
Micron guided fiscal Q3 to **$33.5B revenue, ~81% gross margin, and $19.15 non-GAAP EPS**.

**News-to-model translation:**
- **Revenue:** The earnings power embedded in FY2026 exit-rate numbers is much higher than the market's old Micron template.
- **Margin:** If Micron can print near 81% gross margin even for one quarter, then the normalized margin floor likely moved up versus prior cycles.
- **Multiple:** The guide strengthens the case for using a modestly higher target multiple than old memory trough/peak frameworks, though not enough to justify growth-stock multiples.

### Event 3: HBM4 shipments and the first five-year SCA improve visibility, not just upside narrative
Micron said it has **begun volume shipments of HBM4 for NVIDIA Vera Rubin**, sampled HBM4 16-high, and signed its **first five-year strategic customer agreement**.

**News-to-model translation:**
- **Revenue visibility:** This is tangible evidence that Micron's AI memory position is tied into customer roadmaps over multiple years.
- **Margin floor:** Strategic agreements and qualified HBM products reduce the odds of an abrupt pricing collapse in the very near term.
- **Thesis quality:** This is the strongest rebuttal to the idea that Micron is still just selling undifferentiated commodity bits.

### Event 4: Tight supply beyond 2026 is supportive, but elevated capex remains the main future trap
Management said it expects **DRAM and NAND supply-demand conditions to remain tight beyond calendar 2026**, while fiscal 2026 capex is expected to be **above $25B** and R&D investment to rise meaningfully in fiscal 2027.

**News-to-model translation:**
- **Revenue / margin:** Tight supply supports the base case that Micron's earnings do not collapse immediately after FY2026.
- **Capex / multiple:** Elevated capex is the main reason the market may continue to cap the multiple. If supply eventually loosens into that investment cycle, Micron can still fall into the classic memory overbuild pattern.
- **Bear-case path:** The bear case is not a demand miss next quarter. It is a later normalization combined with a heavy investment base.

## 7. Bear Case

**Operating assumptions:**
- HBM remains an important product line, but competitor ramps from Samsung and SK hynix erode Micron's pricing edge by FY2027.
- Conventional DRAM and NAND pricing weakens as supply catches up and AI spillover demand proves less broad than bulls expect.
- FY2027 revenue falls back to roughly **$90B**.
- Non-GAAP EPS normalizes to about **$32**.

**Valuation assumptions:**
- Target P/E: **8x**
- Fair value: **$256**

**What must be true:**
- HBM supply expands faster than demand.
- AI capex slows enough that customers become less supply-constrained.
- Micron's capex and higher R&D burden pressure returns just as pricing power fades.
- The market decides this was still a peak-memory setup in disguise.

## 8. Base Case

**Operating assumptions:**
- HBM demand remains strong through FY2027 and strategic customer commitments keep Micron's AI memory mix elevated.
- Conventional memory does not boom, but it remains healthier than in prior post-peak downturns because supply is still constrained.
- FY2027 revenue lands around **$110B**.
- Non-GAAP EPS settles around **$48** after normalizing down from the fiscal Q3 run-rate.

**Valuation assumptions:**
- Target P/E: **10x**
- Fair value: **$480**

**What must be true:**
- Industry supply discipline broadly holds through 2027.
- Micron keeps meaningful HBM participation and avoids a sudden pricing reset.
- Normalization happens, but it is gradual, not a cliff.
- Investors accept that the through-cycle Micron multiple should be somewhat higher than in old pure-commodity cycles.

## 9. Bull Case

**Operating assumptions:**
- HBM4 / HBM4E and SCAs meaningfully improve Micron's structural earnings profile.
- AI data-center demand remains supply-constrained well into FY2027, while conventional memory also benefits from higher content and healthier pricing.
- FY2027 revenue reaches roughly **$125B**.
- Non-GAAP EPS reaches about **$60**.

**Valuation assumptions:**
- Target P/E: **11.5x**
- Fair value: **$690**

**What must be true:**
- AI memory demand stays stronger for longer.
- Micron maintains execution lead in HBM4 / HBM4E and converts that into durable mix and margin support.
- Supply discipline remains tighter than prior cycles.
- The market begins to believe Micron deserves a partial structural re-rating.

## 10. What The Current Price Implies

At **$444.27**, the market is no longer pricing Micron as if the HBM story is fake, but it is still pricing a meaningful normalization from the fiscal Q3 exit-rate. At **10x earnings**, today's price implies about **$44.4** of forward EPS. At **9x**, it implies about **$49.4**. That is below the current quarterly run-rate annualization but not far below a reasonable FY2027 base-case normalization.

My read is that the stock currently implies the following:
- FY2027 earnings remain very strong, but below the fiscal Q3 blowout run-rate
- margins normalize from 74.9%-81% to something closer to the high-50s / low-60s rather than collapse to old-cycle trough levels immediately
- the market still assigns a discount because elevated capex and future competitor supply can recreate memory cyclicality

That is why the stock looks **fair, not obviously cheap**. The market is skeptical, but not asleep.

## 11. Provisional Conclusion

**Fair to slightly attractive fundamentally, but not enough margin of safety to call it cheap.**

Micron is trading about **7.4% below** my base fair value of **$480**, which is better than paying peak enthusiasm prices but not a deep discount for a cyclical name whose current margins are historically extreme. The business evidence is excellent: Q2 reset the earnings base higher, Q3 guidance is extraordinary, HBM4 is shipping, supply remains tight, and the balance sheet is strong.

The problem is that the stock has already rerated hard. At this price, you are still depending on the idea that Micron's normalized earnings power has permanently stepped up versus old cycles. I think that is directionally right, but I do not think today's price offers enough protection if the market starts looking through fiscal Q3 and toward post-2026 normalization.

**My judgment:** the thesis is good, the numbers are real, and the market's old memory discount is still partly over-applied — but the stock is now in the zone where patience matters more than urgency. I would rather own Micron than short it here, but I would prefer to add on weakness or after another quarter that confirms the higher floor.

## 12. Thesis Status

**Intact.**

The evidence since the prior Micron framework clearly supports the thesis rather than weakens it:
- Q2 revenue, margin, EPS, and free cash flow all reset higher.
- Q3 guidance shows the strength is continuing, not already fading.
- HBM4 shipment progress and the first five-year SCA improve visibility.
- Tight DRAM and NAND supply beyond 2026 supports the case that this is not an immediate one-quarter peak.

What keeps the thesis from being upgraded into a stronger conviction call is not broken execution; it is price and cycle risk. The business thesis is intact, but the valuation cushion is limited.

## 14. What To Monitor Next

1. **Fiscal Q3 2026 actual results:** Does Micron convert the exceptional guide into another clean beat without signs of margin fade?
2. **CY2027 HBM commitments and customer visibility:** More concrete evidence of multi-year contracted demand would support a higher normalized earnings floor.
3. **Competitor HBM4 / HBM4E ramps:** Samsung and SK hynix execution is the cleanest bear-case pressure point.
4. **FY2027 capex and R&D step-up:** If investment rises faster than the market opportunity, multiple support will weaken.
5. **Conventional DRAM/NAND pricing:** A healthy non-HBM backdrop matters for keeping Micron from becoming a single-product AI trade.
6. **Hyperscaler AI capex commentary:** Any budget fatigue would hit the narrative that current tightness persists well beyond 2026.
7. **Trade and geopolitical developments:** Micron's cycle can still be disrupted by policy and cross-border semiconductor frictions even if demand remains strong.
