# AVGO Analysis

Date: 2026-03-16  
Company: Broadcom Inc.  
Ticker: NASDAQ: AVGO  
Point-in-time price: US$322.16 at the latest available NASDAQ close on 2026-03-13

## 1. Business Classification

Broadcom is best classified as a hybrid mature compounder: AI semiconductors and networking on one side, infrastructure software on the other. It should not be valued as a pure semiconductor stock, because VMware and the broader software portfolio materially change cash-flow stability, margin profile, and multiple durability.

That classification matters because the cleanest primary lens is forward non-GAAP EPS, not GAAP P/E. Acquisition-related amortization materially distorts GAAP earnings, while leverage means an EV-based cross-check is still necessary.

## 2. Thesis

The core thesis is that Broadcom has become one of the few scaled platforms monetizing AI infrastructure from both the compute-network side and the enterprise software side. The semiconductor segment is increasingly tied to hyperscaler AI spend, while infrastructure software provides a lower-volatility cash-flow floor.

At the current price, however, the bet is no longer just "AI is growing." The market is asking investors to underwrite that Broadcom can keep converting AI demand into sustained high-margin earnings growth while the software business remains sticky enough to protect the multiple. If AI growth remains explosive and the software base stays firm, the business can keep compounding. If AI concentration or multiple compression shows up, the stock has much more valuation risk than the business headlines alone suggest.

## 3. Variant Perception

My read is that the market is right to award Broadcom a premium to traditional semis, but it may be overpaying for a very specific outcome: AI continues to ramp hard, software stays stable, and investors remain willing to value Broadcom more like an AI platform than a leveraged hybrid.

The hidden support is the software cash-flow base and Broadcom's unusually strong position in AI networking and custom accelerators. The key misread risk is that investors may be capitalizing a narrow bull path while underweighting customer concentration, AI capex cyclicality, and the fact that Broadcom still carries meaningful net debt. That does not break the thesis. It does make the stock price far less forgiving than the business quality.

## 4. Valuation Lens

Primary lens: forward non-GAAP P/E.  
Secondary cross-check: EV / adjusted EBITDA.

Forward non-GAAP P/E fits because Broadcom is a profitable hybrid compounder whose GAAP EPS is heavily distorted by acquisition accounting. EV / adjusted EBITDA is the right cross-check because the company still has meaningful leverage, and it prevents a misleadingly simple equity-only narrative.

## 6. Recent News And News-To-Model Translation

1. Q1 FY2026 revenue rose 25% year over year to US$14.92 billion, with adjusted EBITDA margin at 68%.
   News-to-model: this supports the idea that Broadcom is not merely seeing AI excitement without earnings conversion. It raises confidence in margin resilience and keeps the base case above a normal mature-chip valuation framework.

2. AI semiconductor revenue reached US$4.1 billion in Q1, and management guided to US$4.4 billion in Q2.
   News-to-model: this raises semiconductor growth assumptions and improves the probability of the bull case. It also increases concentration around hyperscaler AI capex, which is the main reason I keep the bear case alive despite strong current momentum.

3. Infrastructure software revenue reached US$6.7 billion in Q1, up 47% year over year.
   News-to-model: this matters less as a headline growth engine than as a cash-flow stabilizer. It supports a premium multiple versus a pure semi name, but it should not be mistaken for a reason to extrapolate AI-like growth across the whole company.

4. Q2 FY2026 guidance calls for about US$15.0 billion of revenue and adjusted EBITDA margin of about 66%.
   News-to-model: this gives Broadcom a solid near-term floor and keeps 2026 from looking like a one-quarter spike. But the guide is not strong enough by itself to justify the current price unless investors keep capitalizing the company at a very high multiple.

5. Q1 cash from operations was US$6.1 billion and free cash flow was US$6.0 billion, while net debt remains about US$63.1 billion.
   News-to-model: cash generation is strong enough to support the quality case and gradual deleveraging. At the same time, leverage still caps how far I am willing to stretch the valuation multiple versus a net-cash AI leader.

## 7. Bear Case

- Operating assumptions: FY2026 revenue of about US$61.5 billion; adjusted EBITDA margin about 65.5%; non-GAAP EPS about US$6.80
- Valuation assumptions: target P/E 30x
- Fair value: about US$204 per share
- What must be true: AI demand remains good but not exceptional, software behaves mainly as a stabilizer rather than a growth engine, and the market stops paying an AI-platform multiple to a leveraged hybrid

This is not a collapse case. It is the case where Broadcom remains a strong business but the stock is forced back toward a still-premium, but more defensible, valuation.

## 8. Base Case

- Operating assumptions: FY2026 revenue of about US$64.0 billion; adjusted EBITDA margin about 66.5%; non-GAAP EPS about US$7.35
- Valuation assumptions: target P/E 34x
- Fair value: about US$250 per share
- What must be true: AI networking and custom silicon keep expanding, software revenue remains durable, and investors continue to grant Broadcom a premium multiple well above a standard mature semi peer set

This is a constructive operating case, but it still leaves the current stock price looking demanding. In other words, the business can execute well and the stock can still be too expensive.

## 9. Bull Case

- Operating assumptions: FY2026 revenue of about US$68.5 billion; adjusted EBITDA margin about 67.5%; non-GAAP EPS about US$8.35
- Valuation assumptions: target P/E 40x
- Fair value: about US$334 per share
- What must be true: hyperscaler AI spending stays very strong, Broadcom captures more wallet share in custom accelerators and networking, software retention holds, and the market remains willing to value the company like a durable AI platform despite leverage

This is the outcome most consistent with today's share price. That is exactly why I do not think the current setup is attractive from a margin-of-safety perspective.

## 10. What The Current Price Implies

At US$322.16, the market is not merely underwriting a good year. It is underwriting something close to a bull path.

If investors keep paying 40x forward non-GAAP earnings, the current price implies about US$8.05 of EPS. If the multiple slips to 34x, the implied EPS rises to about US$9.48. That means the market is already assuming Broadcom can either deliver very strong AI-driven earnings growth or maintain a far richer multiple than most hybrid, levered companies deserve. On the EV side, the current enterprise value is roughly 37x my base-case adjusted EBITDA, which is a demanding level even for a business of this quality.

## 11. Provisional Conclusion

AVGO looks rich.

The business itself is high quality. AI semiconductor demand is real, software adds durability, and cash generation remains powerful. But the stock is already pricing in a great deal of that good news and appears to require something near a bull-case combination of earnings and multiple support to work from here.

My key underwriting judgment is this: the hidden support is the software layer that makes Broadcom more durable than a pure AI chip bet, but the key risk is that the market is paying as if durability and AI acceleration will both remain unusually strong at the same time. That is too much to underwrite without a better entry.

## 12. Thesis Status

Intact.

The thesis is not broken. Recent results actually reinforce the business case: AI demand remains strong, software is holding up, and cash conversion is high. My concern is price, not operational deterioration. That keeps the thesis at `intact`, not `watch`.

## 14. What To Monitor Next

1. Whether AI semiconductor revenue keeps stepping up beyond the US$4.4 billion Q2 guide, because that is the main driver that can justify current expectations.
2. Whether infrastructure software stays durable after the VMware integration settles into a steadier base, rather than remaining a one-time step-up story.
3. Whether adjusted EBITDA margin can stay in the mid-to-high 60s as revenue mix evolves, because that is central to the premium thesis.
4. Whether net debt begins to fall more visibly, which would make a premium EV-based valuation easier to defend.
5. Whether hyperscaler AI capex concentration becomes a bigger risk, especially if one or two large customers slow custom-accelerator deployment.
