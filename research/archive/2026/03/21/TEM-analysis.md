# TEM Analysis

Company: Tempus AI, Inc.

## 1. Business Classification

Tempus is best treated as a hybrid AI-enabled precision-medicine platform rather than as either a pure diagnostics lab or a pure software company. The business has two economic engines:

- Diagnostics: oncology testing, hereditary testing via Ambry, and algorithm-enhanced clinical workflows
- Data and Applications: data licensing, clinical-trial matching, care-gap tools, pathology and imaging algorithms, and other AI-enabled software layers built on Tempus' multimodal dataset

That classification matters because the right primary lens is forward EV / sales, not P/E. Tempus is only just crossing into positive adjusted EBITDA, GAAP earnings remain distorted by stock compensation, amortization, and acquisition costs, and management is still investing heavily in FDA submissions, cloud infrastructure, and product expansion. A sales-based enterprise-value framework is the cleanest way to underwrite the business while it is still proving operating leverage.

The main revenue drivers are diagnostics volume, reimbursement / ASP improvement, Ambry integration, and continued growth in Data and Applications. The main margin drivers are diagnostic reimbursement, lab efficiency, mix shift toward higher-margin data products, and whether foundation-model / product investments start to scale better against revenue. The main valuation driver is whether investors decide Tempus is becoming a durable hybrid platform with real data moats, or whether they continue to value it as an acquisition-assisted diagnostics story with AI optionality attached.

---

## 2. Thesis

Tempus is worth analyzing because it is starting to move from "AI healthcare concept stock" toward "real operating business with an emerging margin inflection." FY2025 revenue grew 83.4% to $1.27B, Q4 adjusted EBITDA turned positive at $12.9M, and management guided to approximately $1.59B of 2026 revenue with approximately $65M of adjusted EBITDA.

The real bet is not simply that AI in healthcare is a large market. The bet is that Tempus can convert its multimodal clinical-data asset, diagnostics footprint, and expanding applications layer into a higher-quality revenue mix without needing investors to keep paying peak-hype software multiples.

Why that can work:

- Diagnostics is re-accelerating, helped by reimbursement / ASP improvement and Ambry integration.
- Data and Applications still appears to have real momentum: Q4 revenue was $100.4M, TCV exceeded $1.1B, NRR was approximately 126% in 2025, and management expects that business to grow approximately 40% year over year in Q1 2026.
- The company now has enough scale that gross-profit growth can outrun opex growth if execution holds.

Why it can still disappoint:

- Data and Applications margins are not behaving like pure software margins every quarter.
- Tempus remains acquisitive, uses substantial stock compensation, and still has to prove that positive adjusted EBITDA can widen into durable cash generation.
- Regulatory timing, reimbursement, and product commercialization remain meaningful variables.

---

## 3. Variant Perception

The market's biggest analytical mistake with Tempus has been swinging between two bad framings: first treating it like a pure AI / software winner, then swinging too far back toward treating it like a dressed-up diagnostics roll-up. Neither extreme is right.

At $46.96, the stock no longer reflects peak AI euphoria. On roughly 4.9x 2026 EV / sales, investors are no longer paying for a pure-software outcome. But the market is still underwriting a fairly demanding operating path: Diagnostics has to keep improving, Data and Applications has to stay genuinely sticky, and the current adjusted-EBITDA inflection has to prove durable rather than one-quarter optics.

My read is that the stock is now closer to fair than rich, but not cheap enough yet to call a clear valuation disconnect. The hidden support is that the Data and Applications engine looks more durable than a generic "services" multiple would imply. The underwriting risk is that Tempus is still proving that this engine deserves more than a hybrid diagnostics multiple.

---

## 4. Valuation Lens

Primary lens: forward EV / sales on 2026 revenue.

Cross-check: forward EV / adjusted EBITDA on 2026 guidance.

Why this fits:

- Tempus is still in an operating-inflection phase, so earnings-based multiples would create false precision.
- Enterprise value is cleaner than market cap because the company has both cash / marketable securities and meaningful debt.
- EV / sales best captures the question the market is really asking: how much should investors pay for a hybrid diagnostics + data platform that is growing quickly but still proving margin durability.

Why the cross-check matters but should not dominate:

- On 2026 guidance, the stock trades at roughly 120x EV / adjusted EBITDA, which is too high to be useful as a standalone anchor.
- That very fact is useful as a sanity check: it shows how much of the bullish case still depends on margins scaling in 2026 and 2027.
- P/E and FCF yield are not yet good primary tools because GAAP profitability and cash generation are still heavily affected by reinvestment, SBC, and acquisition accounting.

## 5. Current Valuation Snapshot

At $46.96 per share and using approximately 174.3M diluted shares, Tempus has an equity market cap of roughly $8.2B.

Using Q4 cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities of $759.7M, plus year-end 2025 term-loan debt of approximately $202.8M and convertible debt of approximately $208.7M, enterprise value is roughly $7.8B.

Key valuation anchors:

- approximately 6.2x EV / 2025 revenue ($1.27B)
- approximately 4.9x EV / 2026 revenue guidance (~$1.59B)
- approximately 120x EV / 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance (~$65M)

Balance-sheet context: net cash is still modestly positive at roughly $348M, so balance-sheet stress is not the main issue today. The more relevant financing risk is continued dilution and acquisition appetite if Tempus keeps using stock and capital to widen the platform.

## 6. Recent News And News-To-Model Translation

### Q4 2025 results and 2026 guidance

Tempus reported FY2025 revenue of $1.2718B, up 83.4% year over year, and Q4 revenue of $367.2M, up 83.0%. Adjusted EBITDA was positive $12.9M in Q4 and negative $7.4M for the full year. Management guided to approximately $1.59B of 2026 revenue and approximately $65M of adjusted EBITDA.

News-to-model effect:

- raises the base-case revenue floor because management is now explicitly guiding to roughly 25% 2026 growth
- increases confidence that Tempus can cross into sustained positive adjusted EBITDA during 2026
- improves the probability of the Base and Bull cases, because the business is no longer merely promising operating leverage; it has started to show it

### Data and Applications backlog / retention strength

Management disclosed TCV above $1.1B at year-end 2025, up from above $940M a year earlier, while 2025 recognized revenue in that business exceeded $315M. NRR was approximately 126% in 2025, and management said it expects Data and Applications to grow approximately 40% year over year in Q1 2026.

News-to-model effect:

- supports higher-quality revenue visibility in the Data and Applications segment
- improves the case that this segment deserves better-than-diagnostics valuation treatment
- shifts some probability from Bear toward Base / Bull, because the data business appears to be adding contracted value faster than it is recognizing revenue

### Margin drag from new investments

Q4 non-GAAP Data and Applications gross margin fell to 74.5% from 80.3% a year earlier. Management attributed that to start-up costs for the foundation model and lower Applications revenue. Q4 adjusted EBITDA also absorbed accelerated xF FDA-study spending, cloud-infrastructure investments, and the OneOme acquisition.

News-to-model effect:

- caps near-term margin extrapolation
- keeps the Bear case live even if revenue remains strong
- limits how much multiple expansion I am willing to assume before Tempus shows that new AI / applications spend converts into durable gross-profit dollars rather than just more product surface area

### Product and commercial expansion beyond core diagnostics

Recent releases show Tempus expanding the applications / AI layer: a February 2026 collaboration with Median Technologies to integrate AI-powered lung-cancer screening into Pixel; a January 2026 launch of Paige Predict; and a March 2026 expanded multi-year collaboration with Merck noted on the company's news-releases page.

News-to-model effect:

- increases medium-term platform optionality and strengthens the ecosystem story
- helps support Bull-case multiple durability if these products turn into repeatable commercial motion
- does not justify a big near-term revenue step-up by itself, so I treat this as a 2027-plus support variable more than a 2026 number-change variable

## 7. Bear Case

Operating assumptions:

- 2026 revenue reaches only about $1.50B
- Data and Applications growth cools into the mid-teens / low-20s after a strong 2025
- diagnostics reimbursement improvement moderates
- adjusted EBITDA reaches only about $20M because product, cloud, and regulatory spend stays elevated

Valuation assumptions:

- apply 3.8x EV / sales to $1.50B of 2026 revenue
- implied enterprise value: approximately $5.70B
- add net cash of approximately $0.35B
- implied equity value: approximately $6.05B
- divide by approximately 174.3M diluted shares
- fair value: approximately $35 per share

What must be true:

Tempus needs to look less like a scaling hybrid platform and more like an expensive diagnostics / data story whose growth is becoming acquisition-heavy and margin-light. Specifically: data momentum cools, applications monetization stays lumpy, and margin gains fail to keep up with investment intensity.

## 8. Base Case

Operating assumptions:

- 2026 revenue lands around $1.60B, broadly in line with guidance
- diagnostics continues improving on reimbursement, ASP, and Ambry mix
- Data and Applications grows high-20s to low-30s and retains strong contract momentum
- adjusted EBITDA reaches approximately $65M, proving Tempus can keep growing while staying positive on a non-GAAP basis

Valuation assumptions:

- apply 5.6x EV / sales to $1.60B of 2026 revenue
- implied enterprise value: approximately $8.96B
- add net cash of approximately $0.35B
- implied equity value: approximately $9.31B
- divide by approximately 174.3M diluted shares
- fair value: approximately $53 per share

What must be true:

Management has to deliver roughly what it is guiding: sustained diagnostics momentum, real Data and Applications growth, and no major margin reset from continued platform investment. The market does not need to treat Tempus like pure software; it only needs to believe that 2026 is the start of a real multi-year hybrid-platform earnings ramp.

## 9. Bull Case

Operating assumptions:

- 2026 revenue reaches approximately $1.72B
- Data and Applications sustains 35%+ growth, with strong contract conversion and product adoption
- diagnostics algorithms, Pixel, and broader applications start contributing more tangible commercial leverage
- adjusted EBITDA reaches approximately $120M as gross-profit growth outpaces incremental opex more clearly

Valuation assumptions:

- apply 6.6x EV / sales to $1.72B of 2026 revenue
- implied enterprise value: approximately $11.35B
- add net cash of approximately $0.35B
- implied equity value: approximately $11.70B
- divide by approximately 174.3M diluted shares
- fair value: approximately $67 per share

What must be true:

The market needs evidence that Tempus is becoming a genuinely differentiated precision-medicine platform with a durable data moat, not just a fast-growing diagnostics and services business. That evidence would be visible through stronger contract conversion, steadier applications monetization, and clearer EBITDA scaling across 2026.

## 10. What The Current Price Implies

At $46.96, the market is paying roughly 4.9x enterprise value to 2026 revenue guidance and roughly 120x 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance. That is no longer peak-AI software pricing, but it is also not a distressed or washed-out valuation for a business that still needs to prove margin durability.

In practical terms, the current price implies that investors are willing to believe Tempus can hit roughly $1.59B of 2026 revenue and turn adjusted EBITDA positive on a sustained basis, but they are not yet willing to give full credit for a premium data-platform multiple. The market is pricing a business that has moved beyond concept risk but has not yet earned the right to be capitalized like a proven software compounder.

That is why the setup feels fair rather than cheap. The current price already assumes real execution on diagnostics reimbursement, Data and Applications growth, and basic EBITDA scale. What it does not fully assume is a cleaner 2027 story in which applications and AI tools materially improve mix and multiple support. That upside still exists, but it is not large enough today to call the stock obviously mispriced.

---

## 11. Provisional Conclusion

Tempus looks fair, with a mildly constructive skew if 2026 execution confirms the guide. My base-case fair value is approximately $53 per share versus the current $46.96, with Bear / Bull fair values of approximately $35 / $67.

The reason I stop short of a "cheap" call is straightforward: the company has improved meaningfully, but the stock still requires a good portion of that improvement to show up. Investors are already paying for a real hybrid-platform business, not a broken asset. To get materially paid from here, you need Tempus to prove that Data and Applications momentum, reimbursement gains, and positive adjusted EBITDA are durable enough to justify some rerating beyond the current ~4.9x forward EV / sales.

My judgment: Tempus is more decision-useful now than it was at the peak AI-healthcare narrative, but it is still a watch-for-confirmation setup rather than a clear accumulation setup. I would get more interested on either:

- a pullback into the high-$30s / low-$40s without thesis damage, or
- 2026 quarterly evidence that Data and Applications growth and margin stability are holding better than the market currently believes
