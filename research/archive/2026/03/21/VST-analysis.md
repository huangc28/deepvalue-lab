# VST Analysis

Date: 2026-03-21
Company: Vistra Corp.
Ticker: VST
Point-in-time price: ~$149 (March 20 regular-session close $146.02 on ex-dividend date, AH recovery to $149.40)

---

## 1. Business Classification

Vistra is hybrid contracted/merchant power infrastructure. The company pairs one of the largest retail electricity books in the country with a generation fleet spanning nuclear, gas, coal, solar, and battery storage. Cash flow is partially stabilized by customer load, long-term contracts, and nuclear production tax credits, but the outer earnings range still depends on power-market curves, capacity prices, fleet outages, and capital allocation timing.

That classification matters because the right primary lens is enterprise-value based. Leverage is substantial, asset intensity is high, acquisition-driven growth is ongoing, and management itself frames the business around adjusted EBITDA and adjusted free cash flow before growth (FCFbG). Plain P/E distorts the picture because acquisition accounting, hedging, tax structure, and capital allocation can all move the headline number substantially in any quarter.

This analysis is an update from the March 20, 2026 base analysis. The business classification and long-run scenario framework are unchanged. What changed is the current price, one additional nuclear PPA detail (Amazon Web Services at Comanche Peak), a Q4 EPS miss that introduced near-term earnings noise, and a sector-wide sentiment reset driven by rising yields and reduced appetite for the AI-power narrative.

---

## 2. Thesis

Vistra owns some of the most strategically positioned power-generation assets in the continental United States at a moment when structural electricity demand is inflecting upward for the first time in two decades. The company is actively converting that positional advantage into long-duration contracted cash flows — the nuclear PPAs with Meta and Amazon Web Services are the clearest examples — while using operating cash generation to retire debt, repurchase shares, and fund bolt-on growth (Cogentrix).

The stock is worth analyzing now because:

- The March 20 sell-off has reset the price to approximately $149, which is 14% below the base-case fair value and represents a materially better entry point than the $163–$165 range from earlier in March.
- The drivers of the sell-off — ex-dividend mechanics, insider-selling optics, rising Treasury yields, and residual sentiment from a Q4 EPS miss — are not the same as a fundamental deterioration of the business thesis.
- The nuclear PPA book (~3.8 GW contracted with Meta and Amazon) has expanded since the original investment thesis was formed, increasing the quality and durability of the EBITDA base.
- The main underwriting risk is still that Cogentrix integration, 2028 hedge roll, and sequential contract wins all need to execute on schedule for the current earnings step-up to materialize.

---

## 3. Variant Perception

The market is currently pricing in a mix of cyclical nervousness (rising yields hurt utility-adjacent names), AI-power-narrative fatigue, and optics anxiety from insider sales. In my judgment, none of these are fundamental reasons to revisit the EBITDA or FCF framework.

The real variant question is this: the market appears to be treating the sell-off as confirming some underlying worry about Vistra's earnings quality, when the evidence points in the opposite direction. The 2026 EBITDA guidance of $6.8B-$7.6B was reiterated alongside a 2027 FCF/share target of approximately $16 per share — both are stronger numbers than the February guidance set anticipated. The Amazon Web Services Comanche Peak PPA (1,200 MW, 20-year) adds contracted visibility that was not fully on the public record before the Q4 earnings call.

My read: at roughly $149, the market is pricing closer to a "cyclical power stock that might disappoint" rather than a "partly-contracted power infrastructure platform mid-way through a deliberate EBITDA and cash-flow step-up." That gap between the implied price narrative and the visible business evidence is the basis for monitoring this setup for an entry.

## 4. Valuation Lens

Primary lens: EV / EBITDA on normalized 2027 earnings power.

Cross-check: price / adjusted FCFbG.

Why this fits:
- Vistra carries significant acquisition-related debt and capital intensity, so enterprise value is the cleaner anchor
- Management runs the business explicitly around adjusted EBITDA and FCFbG targets and communicates guidance in those terms
- The FCFbG cross-check matters because the company is managed for capital returns: buybacks, dividends, and deleveraging are all funded from FCFbG, and the per-share FCF trajectory is now a published management target ($12.50+ in 2026, ~$16 in 2027)
- P/E is less useful here because acquisition accounting (Cogentrix), hedging gains and losses, nuclear PTC treatment, and capital structure changes can make quarterly EPS noisy and non-comparable

## 5. Current Valuation Snapshot

Using approximately $149 per share as the point-in-time price, with approximately 337M diluted shares, the equity market cap is roughly $50.2B. Adding year-end 2025 net debt of approximately $17.4B, enterprise value is roughly $67.6B.

Key valuation anchors:

- ~9.4x EV / 2026 EBITDA midpoint ($7.2B guidance midpoint)
- ~8.9x EV / 2027 EBITDA midpoint opportunity (~$7.6B)
- ~8.6% FCFbG yield on 2026 guidance (>$12.50 × 337M ≈ $4.2B FCFbG / $50.2B equity)
- ~9.3x P / 2027 FCFbG (~$16 × 337M ≈ $5.4B / $50.2B equity)

These compare favorably to the March 20 valuation at $163.69: 10.1x 2026 EBITDA and 7.8% FCFbG yield. The sell-off has moved the stock from near-fair-value pricing to a more interesting setup relative to the base case.

Balance sheet context: management is targeting approximately 2.3x net debt / EBITDA by year-end 2027. The current implied leverage at $7.2B 2026 EBITDA is approximately 2.4x, which is on track for that path.

## 6. Recent News And News-To-Model Translation

### March 20, 2026 sell-off

VST dropped approximately 12.6% on March 20, closing at $146.02 before recovering to $149.40 in after-hours trading. The primary drivers were: the ex-dividend date (quarterly dividend of $0.228/share reduced the opening price mechanically), disclosed insider sales by CEO Jim Burke and EVP Stephanie Zapata Moore earlier in March, rising 10-year Treasury yields (4.28% to 4.39%), a sector-wide rotation out of utility-adjacent AI-power names, and residual overhang from the Q4 earnings miss.

News-to-model effect:
- This is a price event, not a model event. The 2026 EBITDA and FCFbG guidance were not changed, no new capex was added, and no contract was withdrawn.
- The insider sales affect optics but are normal insider-trading-window activity and do not signal a thesis break.
- The direction of model impact is neutral: no model variable was changed by the sell-off itself. What did change was the price, which improved the risk-reward.

### Amazon Web Services nuclear PPA at Comanche Peak

Vistra disclosed during the Q4 2025 earnings call that it has signed a 20-year PPA with Amazon Web Services covering 1,200 MW of nuclear generation at Comanche Peak in ERCOT. Combined with the Meta agreements (2,176 MW nuclear + 433 MW PJM upgrades), Vistra now has approximately 3.8 GW of contracted nuclear generation under long-term agreements. Management estimates that nuclear PPAs, upon full ramp, will deliver approximately 25% annual adjusted FCFbG accretion.

News-to-model effect:
- Increases contracted revenue visibility and shifts more of the EBITDA base from merchant optionality toward contracted durability.
- Strengthens the case for a mid-cycle rather than cyclical-peak valuation multiple — a fully contracted nuclear cash-flow stream deserves a steadier, lower-discount-rate treatment than spot merchant generation.
- Also modestly caps pure merchant upside, because converting optionality into fixed-price agreements removes upside from higher spot prices.
- Affected scenarios: Base and Bull (improves durability in both; marginally caps the tail of the bull case).

### Q4 2025 earnings miss on EPS and revenue

Q4 2025 adjusted EPS came in at $2.18 versus a consensus estimate of approximately $2.45. Revenue for the quarter was $4.58B against consensus of approximately $5.75B. Full-year 2025 ongoing operations adjusted EBITDA was approximately $5.9B-$6.2B depending on segment consolidation methodology, broadly on target with original guidance.

News-to-model effect:
- The quarterly miss adds near-term EPS uncertainty but does not change the annual EBITDA run-rate or the 2026-2027 guidance trajectory.
- Management reiterated the 2026 guidance of $6.8B-$7.6B EBITDA and introduced the 2027 FCF/share target of approximately $16 per share.
- The bear scenario probability increases modestly because a quarterly miss introduces the possibility that hedging timing or margin assumptions were off. However, full-year 2025 EBITDA was broadly achieved, which limits the read-through to the forward outlook.
- Affected scenario: Bear (modestly higher probability weight given near-term EPS noise).

### 2027 FCF/share guidance introduction

Vistra management introduced a 2027 FCF/share target of approximately $16 on the Q4 call, alongside the existing 2026 >$12.50 guidance. This is a stronger forward cash-flow signal than the prior model assumed.

News-to-model effect:
- At ~$16 FCF/share × 337M shares ≈ $5.4B of FCFbG in 2027, which implies EBITDA of approximately $8.5B-$9.0B assuming 60% conversion — potentially above the high end of the prior bull case.
- This does not change the fair value scenarios materially because the step-up is largely already captured by the bull-case EBITDA assumption of $8.3B. But it does add confidence to the base-to-bull range.
- Affected scenario: Base / Bull (raises confidence floor on cash generation).

## 7. Bear Case

Operating assumptions:

- 2027 EBITDA settles around $6.8B because additional contract wins arrive slowly, Cogentrix encounters integration friction or regulatory delay, and the market re-focuses on leverage and execution risk
- Q4 EPS weakness carries into early 2026 and reinforces investor concerns about near-term profitability
- Rising yields keep the utility-adjacent multiple compressed at a time when Vistra needs credit for its quality improvement story

Valuation assumptions:

- Apply 8.6x EV / EBITDA to approximately $6.8B of 2027 EBITDA
- Implied enterprise value: approximately $58.5B
- Less net debt of approximately $17.4B
- Implied equity value: approximately $41.1B
- Using approximately 337M diluted shares: fair value of approximately $122 per share

What must be true:

Vistra needs to look more like a fully priced cyclical power name than a steadily improving infrastructure compounder. Specifically: Cogentrix integration stumbles, no meaningful new contract wins, and the market assigns a discount for the leverage overhang and near-term EPS volatility.

- Less net debt of approximately $17.4B

- Using approximately 337M diluted shares: fair value of approximately $174 per share

What must be true:

Retail execution stays strong, fleet operations remain reliable, Cogentrix closes on schedule, and more evidence appears that signed contracts durably lift base profitability rather than front-loading excitement.

## 9. Bull Case

Operating assumptions:

- Vistra exits 2027 with earnings power closer to $8.3B-$8.5B of EBITDA; the $16 FCF/share target proves achievable or conservative
- Cogentrix integrates cleanly and begins showing accretion by mid-2027; load growth in ERCOT and PJM stays structurally elevated
- Additional nuclear or gas contracts are signed beyond the already announced Meta and AWS agreements, and the market re-rates Vistra as a structurally contracted infrastructure platform

Valuation assumptions:

- Apply 10.8x EV / EBITDA to approximately $8.3B of 2027 EBITDA
- Implied enterprise value: approximately $89.6B
- Less net debt of approximately $17.4B
- Implied equity value: approximately $72.3B
- Using approximately 337M diluted shares: fair value of approximately $214 per share

What must be true:

The market needs proof that Vistra deserves to be capitalized as a structurally tighter, more contracted, and more durable power-infrastructure platform rather than as a late-cycle beneficiary of AI electricity enthusiasm. That proof arrives via sequential contract wins, visible EBITDA step-ups, and continued deleveraging through 2027.

## 10. What The Current Price Implies

At approximately $149, the stock is pricing something noticeably closer to the bear-to-midpoint range rather than the base case. The market is currently underwriting approximately 9.4x 2026 EBITDA midpoint and approximately 11.9x 2026 FCFbG midpoint — both well below the more demanding multiples seen at the $163-$165 range earlier in March.

What the current price appears not to be crediting:
- The improving quality of the EBITDA base from nuclear PPAs (3.8 GW contracted with Meta and Amazon, delivering an estimated 25% FCFbG accretion on ramp)
- The $16 FCF/share 2027 management target, which if achieved means the stock is trading at approximately 9.3x forward FCFbG today
- The Cogentrix gas-generation acquisition, which adds roughly 5.5 GW of modern capacity at a price management characterized as approximately 7.25x 2027 EBITDA contribution — itself cheaper than the current multiple the market is assigning to Vistra

What the current price appears to be pricing in: execution risk (the Q4 miss), macro multiple compression (rising yields), and narrative fatigue around the AI-power trade. Structurally, those concerns feel more like a sentiment-driven mark-to-market than a model revision.

## 11. Provisional Conclusion

Vistra looks more interesting at $149 than it did at $163-$165. The business thesis is intact, the contracted nuclear book has grown, and the 2027 FCF/share target at $16 per share implies a forward FCFbG yield of approximately 10.7% on today's equity price — which is not the multiple you'd expect to pay for a steadily improving power-infrastructure platform.

That said, the stock is not yet clearly cheap by the strict definition (it trades above the bear-case fair value of $122 × 1.1 = $134 threshold). The provisionally fair-to-improving-risk-reward conclusion reflects:
- Base case fair value of $174 represents approximately 17% upside from $149
- Bull case fair value of $214 represents approximately 44% upside
- The main entry-timing argument is that the sell-off was not driven by fundamental deterioration, so the new price level is better evidence of a risk-reward reset than a thesis break

My practical view: this is now a name worth watching actively for an entry at or below $149, with more conviction building if the stock holds the $145-$150 zone or gives a technical confirmation of a floor. I would size a position here rather than waiting for an obvious oversold signal that might not arrive before the stock recovers.

## 13. Technical Entry Status

**Neutral, approaching a more constructive zone.**

As of the March 20 close, the RSI (14-day) is approximately 41-43, which is approaching but has not yet reached the conventional oversold threshold of 30. The stock is now below the 5-day, 50-day, and 200-day simple moving averages, with the MACD in negative territory — a short-term bearish technical posture overall.

The technical picture does not yet confirm a floor, which is why the entry status is neutral rather than favorable — the better entry zone would be confirmed by a stabilization and recovery from the current lows rather than a catch-a-falling-knife approach.

Key technical levels:

- Whether the March 20-21 low holds and the stock stabilizes in the $145-$155 zone, which would confirm the sell-off was sentiment-driven rather than tracking new fundamental information
- Cogentrix acquisition timeline: FERC, DOJ/HSR, and state regulatory approvals are required; any delay beyond mid-to-late 2026 expected close would be a negative model variable
- Additional long-duration gas or nuclear contract announcements beyond the currently confirmed Meta and AWS agreements

- Whether management tracks toward the $16 FCF/share 2027 target or if Q1 2026 results show any guidance pull-back
- Net debt / EBITDA trajectory: management is targeting approximately 2.3x by year-end 2027; leverage staying above 2.5x for longer would cap multiple expansion
